/ 00  Paved States Second Edition

The 2026 Paved States × Haworth DesignLab installation takes over THE MART's lobby and Suite 121 with work from a continental roster of emerging designers across furniture, lighting, sculpture, jewelry, and the in-between.

The space includes a curated concept store of objects and editions, a Sixtysix-curated bookstore and newsstand stocked with the most interesting design and lifestyle magazines, and an analog lounge featuring curated playlists, guest DJ sessions, and a place to sit and listen.

Throughout the week, the space hosts conversations with curator Patricia Urquiola, Chris Force, and the exhibiting designers themselves. Expect a few unexpected workshops along the way.

Panel Discussion

Ask Anything with Patricia Urquiola

Patricia Urquiola · Curator & Designer · with the exhibiting designers of Paved States

What if you could ask Patricia anything? The exhibiting designers of Paved States have prepared their most probing questions, and guests are invited to do the same. An open exchange on practice, process, and the state of design now. Come with a question.

About the Presenter
Patricia Urquiola is one of the most influential designers working today. Born in Spain, trained under Achille Castiglioni at Politecnico di Milano, she founded her own studio in 2001 and has served as Creative Director of Cassina since 2015. Her work spans furniture, architecture, and interiors for clients including B&B Italia, Moroso, Flos, Louis Vuitton, and Haworth, and is held in the permanent collections of MoMA, the V&A, and the Vitra Design Museum.

Workshop · Jewelry

Dental Couture with Secret of Manna

Tim Nicholson aka Secret of Manna · Celebrity Jeweler & Artist

A former video game designer turned metalsmith, Tim Nicholson built his practice transforming pop culture references into experimental wearable art. At Paved States, he walks you through his design process from concept to material selection, then guides you in creating your own piece. Each participant is custom molded on-site and leaves fitted for a one-of-a-kind silver tooth cap, handcrafted with Secret of Manna and delivered after the event.

About the Presenter
Tim Nicholson aka Secret of Manna is a celebrity jeweler and artist who traded video game design for gold and silver. His practice turns pop culture artifacts—mouse cursors, bread clips, internet relics—into sculptural jewelry that blurs the line between luxury and play.

Panel · Practice

The Curiosity Framework — a panel with Benjamin Edgar

Benjamin Edgar, Designer · Moderated by Chris Force, Editor-in-Chief, Sixtysix

Benjamin Edgar in conversation with Chris Force, Editor-in-Chief of Sixtysix, on building a practice without a rulebook. The conversation digs into how emerging designers develop a point of view, navigate the industry on their own terms, and find a framework for making work that matters.

About the Presenter
Benjamin Edgar is a Chicago-based designer working across industrial design, contemporary culture, sustainable goods, and education. He founded Boxed Water as an alternative to plastic bottles, co-wrote the culture blog The Brilliance alongside Chuck Anderson and Virgil Abloh, and runs An Object Company, an ongoing project producing objects in materials from cotton to marble, each one balancing the useful with the useless.

Workshop · Leather

Wallet Making with Noel Mercado

Noel Mercado · Chicago Designer & Artist

In this hands-on leather wallet workshop, Noel guides participants through hand-stitching and stamping on pre-cut leather patterns. Each attendee constructs their own custom wallet or cardholder, personalizing it with custom letter and number stamps—initials, names, whatever feels right—while drawing on Noel's distinctive approach to craft and cultural storytelling.

About the Presenter
Noel Mercado is a Chicago-based multimedia artist that transforms found and salvaged objects into handcrafted artworks, furniture, and wearable pieces that explore the cultural meaning of everyday materials.

Workshop · Floral

Second Nature — A Floral Workshop with Nerine

Sinead Cleary & Liz Topp, Founders, Nerine

In this hands-on workshop, participants explore the fundamentals of custom floral design guided by the founders of Nerine. Each attendee composes their own arrangement to take home in a silver vessel, while learning how flora, form, and texture function as design elements in everyday spaces.

About the Presenters
Sinead Cleary and Liz Topp are the founders of Nerine, a Chicago experimental floral studio known for arrangements as sculptural as they are natural.

Panel · Reframing Modernism

Beyond the Icon — Artists Reframe Midcentury Modern Furniture

Noel Mercado & Daniel Overbey, Norman Teague Studio · Artists & Designers

A critical conversation on reinterpreting iconic midcentury modern furniture through contemporary, culturally grounded perspectives. Drawing from their practices, both designers explore how modernist design histories can be reimagined to better reflect lived experience, identity, and community. Together, they consider the Farnsworth House as a space for inclusivity, dialogue, and reinterpretation.

On the Panel
Noel Mercado will discuss his recent solo exhibition at the Edith Farnsworth House, examining how his work engages modernist forms while incorporating narratives rooted in Latino culture and craftsmanship. Daniel Overbey of Norman Teague Studio will share insights from the studio's practice and preview their upcoming 2027 exhibition at the Farnsworth House, highlighting design as a tool for storytelling, social engagement, and cultural visibility within the Black design tradition.
